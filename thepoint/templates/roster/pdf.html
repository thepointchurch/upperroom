a note to our song leaders

“…whenever you come together, each of you has a psalm, has a teaching, has a tongue, has a revelation, has an interpretation. Let all things be done for edification”

Eugene Peterson puts it well when he paraphrases the apostle Paul:

“When you gather for worship, each one of you be prepared with something that will be useful for all: sing a hymn, teach a lesson, tell a story, lead a prayer, provide an insight”
“Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly in all wisdom, teaching and admonishing one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing with grace in your hearts”
“…but be filled with the Spirit, speaking to one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing and making melody in your heart to the Lord, giving thanks always for all things to God the Father in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ…”

Singing in Christian assemblies makes an important contribution to the overall objective of gathering on the Lord’s Day to “…consider one another in order to stir up love and good works…” (Hebrews 10:19-25). According to Paul (see the texts above), singing is a medium of teaching and admonishing one another, as well as a vehicle of expressing praise (thanksgiving) to God … whether rendered as a solo, antiphonally (parts or responsive) or congregationally (in unison).

In setting apart a block of fifteen minutes devoted to singing in our Sunday assemblies, song leaders have an opportunity to design and deliver a meaningful period of edification and praise.

In preparing for leading the church in song in 2019, I want to encourage you to devote as much thought and planning to that important role as you would if you were preparing to teach a lesson or lead communion. Consider developing a theme (whether connected to the morning’s lesson or the Lord’s Supper, or you may decide to develop your own theme) and select your songs thoughtfully and purposefully to develop that theme. You might choose to connect the songs with a brief commentary or short and relevant Bible readings or prayers to support the theme.

Ask yourself critical questions like: “What am I trying to teach?” or “What aspect of God’s character or work do I want to direct the congregation in praise and thanksgiving for?” “What are the songs that will help develop this theme most logically and meaningfully?” “How can I connect each song, so they flow together?” “Are we achieving a balance of old and new songs?” Please spend time in prayer over your preparations.

Thank you for blessing the church with your gifts and honouring God through your efforts!
